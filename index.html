Global Shark Attacks Over the Years

Despite their often sensationalized portrayal in the media, global shark attacks are relatively rare. Sharks are vital to marine ecosystems, playing a crucial role in maintaining the health of our oceans. In fact, most shark species pose no threat to humans.

On average, there are only about 80 to 100 reported shark attacks worldwide each year, with a relatively low percentage being fatal. The International Shark Attack File (ISAF) diligently tracks and compiles data on shark attacks to gain a better understanding of these incidents and enhance safety measures.

Many shark attacks are believed to be cases of mistaken identity, where sharks confuse humans with their typical prey. Swimmers and surfers are most commonly involved in these incidents due to their presence in the water. To minimize the risk of shark encounters, experts recommend adhering to safety guidelines, such as avoiding areas known for high shark activity, avoiding swimming alone, and staying informed about local shark advisories.
