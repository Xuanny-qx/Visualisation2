Global Shark Attacks Over the Years

Despite their often sensationalized portrayal in the media, global shark attacks are relatively rare. Sharks are vital to marine ecosystems, playing a crucial role in maintaining the health of our oceans.

The International Shark Attack File (ISAF) diligently tracks and compiles data on shark attacks to gain a better understanding of these incidents and enhance safety measures.

Geography|Dot Map

The map vividly charts the geographical footprint of shark attacks. Each dot on the map symbolizes an individual attack, painting a comprehensive picture of where these encounters occur.

Trend|Line Chart

The dynamic line chart elegantly illustrates the evolving pattern of shark attacks over time.

Attack Type|Pie Chart

The pie chart provides a visual breakdown of shark attacks by type, including categories like boating, provoked, sea disaster, and unprovoked. Additionally, it sheds light on cases labeled as 'questionable,' indicating scenarios where the reason for the shark attack remains a mystery.

Fatality|Pie Chart

The pie chart visually represents the distribution of shark attack incidents, distinguishing between those that resulted in a fatality (Y) and those that were non-fatal (N).

Activity&Fatality|Bubble Chart

This chart displays the top shark attack activities by frequency, with circles representing each activity. The size of the circles indicates how often the activity occurred, and the color distinguishes between fatal (red) and non-fatal (blue) incidents. It shows the most common activities and their fatality status.
